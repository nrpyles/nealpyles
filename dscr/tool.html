Questions investors always ask.

What is DSCR and why does it matter?

Debt service coverage ratio: the monthly rent divided by the full monthly payment (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, HOA). At 1.0 the property pays for itself and most lenders will fund the deal. At 1.25 you unlock the best pricing tiers. Your tax returns, W2s, and personal debt never enter the math.

What are the typical minimums?

20% or more down, a credit score around 620 to 680 or better, and 3 to 6 months of payments in reserves after closing. Options exist below a 1.0 DSCR at adjusted pricing. Loan amounts usually run $75,000 to $3 million.

Why would I ever accept a prepayment penalty?

Because it buys a lower rate. A 5 year penalty structure typically prices well below a no penalty loan. If you plan to hold the property past the penalty window, the penalty never bites and the cheaper rate wins. If you plan to sell or refinance early, the "cost to exit" comparison on page 1 shows which structure actually costs less for your timeline.

Will the lender use my rent number?

The qualifying rent is set by the appraiser's market rent schedule (Form 1007), not your estimate or even the current lease alone. The market rent estimate in this analysis is a good preview, but plan around the appraisal.

Can I buy in an LLC? What about short term rentals?

Yes, most DSCR lenders close in an LLC with a personal guarantee, which is how most investors hold rentals. Short term rentals work with many lenders using 12 month revenue history or a market rent analysis, usually with a bigger DSCR cushion.
